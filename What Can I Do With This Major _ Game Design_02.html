Strategies

  • Double major in an area that you're interested in that will support your game design interests:

    • If you are interested in programming and engineering, consider double majoring in Computer Science

    • If you are interested in level design

      , consider double majoring

      in Interior Design

    • If you are interested in economy design

      , consider double majoring in

      Finance

    • If you are interested in narrative design

      , consider double majoring

      in English (Writing)

  • Take programming classes to increase your understanding and proficiency in game logic and programming languages

  • Become an active participant HPU's GAMbassador program

  • Learn to use many different game engines such as Unreal Engine, Unity, Stencyl, GameMaker, Construct, etc.

  • Understand that there is rarely an entry-level "game designer" position - most people start their game career in other more specific design roles such as Level Designer, UI Designer, Audio Designer, QA, etc

  • Develop a strong understanding of the cycle of prototyping, play testing, and iteration

  • Learn basic game programming skills using visual scripting methods (such as Blueprints)

  • Participate in game jams and game design competitions

  • Develop a broad skill set but specialize in one area of game design/development

  • Develop an online game design portfolio using Behance, Wix, or any other online portfolio site

  • Join University student clubs relevant to your major (Video Game Development Club, Video Game Club, etc)

  • Join International Game Developers Association (IGDA) or other relevant professional organization(s)

  • Attend Game Dev, Game Engine, or VR Meetups

  • Make, finish, and publish games

Writing / Narrative

  • Game Writing

  • Narrative Design

  • Technical Writing

  • Game Journalism

Strategies

  • Double major in areas such as Popular Culture & Media Production (POP), Journalism (JOU), English, or other related major

  • Enroll in GAM 3342 - Narrative & Interactive Fiction

  • Enroll in complementary courses such as POP 2231 - Writing for Media Production, or Journalism courses.

  • Design and develop digital prototypes ranging from text-based engines such as Twine, to AAA engines like Unreal Engine

  • Game Writer Challenge: make a list of 100 different ways to say, "I'm not going to buy that axe today."

  • Rework your favorite novels or movies into branching narrative games

  • Keep and maintain a journal and use on a daily basis

  • Learn to write in as many different styles and for as many different genres and audiences as you can

  • Learn to code: as a writer or narrative designer in games, you will be expected to use game engines proficiently
